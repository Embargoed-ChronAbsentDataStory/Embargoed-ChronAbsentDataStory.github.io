Mark Zuckerberg and Priscilla Chan's Commitment to Educational Innovation

Developing new software for K-12 schools. Investing in groundbreaking ed-tech startups. Donating tens of millions of dollars to schools experimenting with fresh approaches to customizing the classroom experience.

All reflect Mark Zuckerberg and Priscilla Chan's passion for advancing educational equity through technology. The U.S. Department of Education shares that commitment and recently built the College Scorecard, a digital tool that gives students and their advocates a new way to find and compare colleges across the factors that matter to them.

The College Scorecard API

Underlying the College Scorecard tool is the College Scorecard API, which provides access to a massive, unprecedented dataset on U.S. colleges.

For the first time, developers can quickly and easily retrieve information on students' outcomes at specific colleges, including former students' earnings; graduates' student debt; and borrowers' repayment rates. This data is also available for various sub-groups, like first generation and Pell students.

Others have already used the API to create innovative new resources for students and to drive transparency around college choice. We think Facebook would be interested in doing the same.

Data Widget

The idea: an optional data widget universities could include on their profile pages that would display key metrics drawn from the College Scorecard dataset.

Stronger Messages About Value

Universities would have another way to market and substantiate the value of their education, using data from an authority the public finds more trustworthy.

Richer Browsing

Users would find more value in browsing university profiles on Facebook. Quick facts about student costs and outcomes make it easier to compare schools of interest.

College Chatbot

The idea: a chatbot designed to answer students' questions about the costs and outcomes of college. It would pull from the College Scorecard data to describe and compare colleges to interested users.

A Vital Service Made More Accessible

Guidance counselors struggle to meet students' needs for information about their college options. The chatbot could support them by providing instantenous, student-driven counseling based on the best available evidence about colleges.

Deeper Market Penetration

The chatbot could enhance the practical value of using Facebook for key audiences, including young people and other prospective students. Additionally, as Facebook asserts greater influence in the ed tech sector, the chatbot could prove a critical tool to a wide swath of stakeholders.

Scorecard Data In Virtual Reality

The idea: include Scorecard data in relevant virtual reality experiences (e.g., virtual campus tours).

Advance VR's Value to Education

Facebook is an industry leader in promoting the applications of VR to education. Allowing virtual campus tour participants, for example, to interact with Scorecard data within the experience itself would better exploit the technology while leaving students more informed.
